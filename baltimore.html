A Breif Overview

Baltimore, Maryland, stands as a vibrant testament to resilience, diversity, and innovation. This bustling metropolis, affectionately known as "Charm City," pulsates with energy, offering a dynamic mix of history, culture, and urban flair. At the heart of Baltimore lies the iconic Inner Harbor, a bustling waterfront district that serves as a focal point for both locals and visitors. Here, historic ships dock alongside modern attractions like the National Aquarium and the Maryland Science Center, creating a vibrant tapestry of past and present. The city's rich history is palpable in its architecture, from the elegant rowhouses of Mount Vernon to the towering spires of the Bromo Seltzer Tower, each telling a story of Baltimore's storied past and enduring spirit.

Beyond its historic landmarks, Baltimore boasts a thriving cultural scene that delights residents and visitors alike. World-class museums, including the Baltimore Museum of Art and the Walters Art Museum, showcase an impressive array of artwork spanning centuries and continents. The city's theaters and music venues come alive with performances ranging from classical symphonies to cutting-edge theater productions, while its eclectic neighborhoods offer a tantalizing array of dining options, from upscale eateries to beloved local haunts. With its vibrant street art scene, lively festivals, and diverse communities, Baltimore captivates with its unmistakable charm and boundless creativity.

Popular Restaurants

Helmand

"The Helmand has helped introduce and popularize Afghan Cuisine in America within the last 20 years. The Helmand was named after the owner's first born son, as well as in honor of the famed Helmand River that runs through the predominantly dry country of Afghanistan, bringing vitality and nourishment to it's people. Much like this river, The Helmand of Baltimore provides their guests with enjoyment in the form of exquisite food prepared with care and dedication. First established in the autumn of 1989, The Helmand is proudly celebrating 32 years!"

-Helmand

Shopping

Cross Street Market

"One of the USA's oldest public markets, built in 1846 to serve the growing area of South Baltimore. In 2019, Cross Street Market completed a full renovation."

-Trip Advisor


Federal Hill

"Federal Hill was a defensive stronghold during the War of 1812 and replica cannons sit atop the neighborhood’s namesake hill in honor of its role as a military outpost. Now, Federal Hill is characterized by historic brick row homes and numerous locally-owned shops and restaurants. It’s home to both newcomers and families that have lived here for generations. Its charm combines 19th century history with beautiful architecture and ample entertainment."

-Visit Balitmore


Fell's Point

"One of the oldest neighborhoods in Baltimore, Fell’s Point was once a bustling shipbuilding port, and has served as the home to jazz singer Billie Holiday and abolitionist Frederick Douglass. Its visage has remained largely unchanged since its founding—picture Belgian block streets, waterfront restaurants and cozy boutiques just a few blocks east of the Inner Harbor. Spend a day during your next visit to Baltimore exploring historic Fell’s Point. There are so many things to see, eat, do and enjoy in this walkable neighborhood."

-Visit Baltimore

The Bluebird Cocktail Room

"The Bluebird Cocktail Room is a literary-inspired bar in Baltimore’s Hampden neighborhood, influenced by the common literary themes like decadence, idealized pasts, and frivolous lifestyles expressed by the Lost Generation and authors like Virginia Woolf, Ernest Hemingway, and F. Scott Fitzgerald. This influence can be found in every aspect of the Bluebird, from its plush golden seats, deep blue walls, and ornate glassware to the intricacy used in developing and re-creating both classic and signature cocktails and the bar’s artisanal ice program. The Bluebird operates daily from 5pm – 2am, serving happy hour from 5pm-7pm Tuesday-Friday, all day Sunday and Monday. We also serve food until 1am seven nights a week. Typically, the Bluebird adheres to a no reservation, no standing policy."

-Visit Baltimore
