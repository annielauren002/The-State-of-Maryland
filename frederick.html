A Breif Overview

Frederick, Maryland, nestled amid the scenic beauty of Frederick County, exudes a captivating blend of small-town charm and cultural vibrancy. With its rich history dating back to its founding in 1745, Frederick boasts a well-preserved historic district characterized by brick sidewalks, 18th and 19th-century architecture, and quaint storefronts. Visitors can wander through the charming streets of downtown Frederick, where boutique shops, art galleries, and cozy cafes beckon, offering a glimpse into the city's storied past and vibrant present. Historic landmarks such as the National Museum of Civil War Medicine and the Schifferstadt Architectural Museum provide insight into Frederick's role in shaping the nation's history.

Beyond its historic charm, Frederick offers a thriving cultural scene that captivates residents and visitors alike. The city's arts community is vibrant and diverse, with galleries showcasing works by local artists and theaters hosting dynamic performances ranging from classic plays to contemporary productions. Throughout the year, Frederick comes alive with festivals and events celebrating its heritage, from the annual Frederick Festival of the Arts to the lively festivities of In the Street, where downtown Frederick transforms into a bustling street festival featuring music, food, and local artisans. Surrounded by rolling hills and picturesque countryside, Frederick is not just a destination, but an experience, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in its rich history, cultural offerings, and natural beauty.

Things To Do

Ghost Tours of Historic Frederick

"Discover Frederick’s past as we navigate through the labyrinth of Graves, Crypts and Monuments of Historic Mount Olivet Cemetery. A 90-minute excursion through Mount Olivet’s hallowed grounds to pay respect to some of Maryland’s most famous and noteworthy figures. Final resting place of Francis Scott Key; Maryland's first governor; Civil War heroine, Barbara Fritchie; and 700 Confederate soldiers.."

-Trip Advisor


Gambrill State Park

"Gambrill State Park, located west of Frederick, provides miles of hiking, mountain biking, and horse trails with stunning overlooks of the surrounding area. A 34-campsite, four-camper cabin campground is available seasonally. See beautiful views from three stone overlooks, picnic shelters for rent, and the historic Tearoom, an event facility available for reservations. Some facilities are handicapped accessible."

-Visit Frederick


National Museum of Civil War Medicine

"Dedicated to telling the medical story of the Civil War - a story of care and healing, courage and devotion amidst death and destruction, while laying the foundation for modern medical care.
Explore the Exhibits: Follow in the footsteps of soldiers and surgeons to discover the harsh conditions, personal sacrifices, and brilliant innovations of Civil War medicine, innovations that continue to save lives today."

-Vist Frederick
