Shopping

Westfield Annapolis Mall

"Located in Maryland's state capital, three miles from the city's historic waterfront and situated within the nation's 4th largest market place in the Baltimore-Washington, DC corridor. Featuring over 200 stores including Nordstrom, Macy's, Lord & Taylor, JCPenney and Sears. Westfield Annapolis is the Chesapeake Bay area's preferred shopping, dining and entertainment destination. Shopping Center Hours: Monday - Saturday: 10:00am-9:00pm Sunday: 11:00am - 7:00pm. Department store and restaurant hours may vary."

-Visit Annapolis


Downtown Annapolis

"The downtown Annapolis area is filled with about 90 stores for shopping. We invite you to visit downtown Annapolis take a stroll and shop at the many local businesses on Main Street, Church Circle, Maryland Avenue, West Street, Market Space, Dock Street, and Compromise Street.. You also can use the links below to plan your shopping visit to Annapolis or shop online. Use the map to see them all."

-Downtown Annapolis Partnership


Towne Center

"A unique blend of historic charm, nautical aesthetic, modern amenities and community-focused design make Annapolis Town Center a popular destination that truly brings people together. Visitors and locals alike enjoy a diverse mix of retail, specialty stores and restaurants offering cuisine from around the world. We invite you to explore, gather and stay awhile. Annapolis Town Center truly shines as the gem of Annapolis, offering year-round entertainment that includes lively outdoor concerts, festive holiday experiences, and unique seasonal celebrations."

-Annapolis Towne Center


Drinks

The 1747 Pub

"The 1747 Pub is in the original kitchen and ‘Hat Shop’ of William Reynolds. It has stone and brick walls, walk-in fireplace, low ceilings, brick floors, and the original stairwell from the 1737 construction. In the 1950s, the cellar was the Childrens’ Library for the Anne Arundel Public Library System. A cozy spot for lunch, dinner, happy hour, or a drink with friends where guests get a glimpse into history."

-The 1747 Pub
